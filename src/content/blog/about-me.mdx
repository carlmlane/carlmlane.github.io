I'm Carl — VP of Engineering & Product Development at Marqii, based in San Francisco. This blog is where I'll be writing about engineering leadership, software architecture, and the things I'm figuring out along the way. But first, a bit about who I am.

## The Scenic Route

My path into engineering leadership was anything but linear. I was born in the Bay Area, grew up partly in San Diego, and after high school I moved to Madrid, Spain — a chapter that left a lasting mark on how I see the world.

Before I ever thought about software, I was obsessed with spaceflight and aviation. I studied aerospace engineering at Saint Louis University's Parks College, and that fascination with complex systems never really left me. It just shifted from rockets to codebases.

I've been using computers since 1990 and writing code since 1998. I spent nearly a decade at Wix, and now I'm scaling the engineering org at Marqii. My journey from support rep to VP taught me something I carry with me every day: **the best engineering leaders never lose touch with the people they build for.**

## What I Believe

A few things I've come to believe about building software and leading teams:

- **Small, high-IQ teams beat large, bureaucratic ones every time.** Keep teams lean, hire for curiosity, and get out of their way.
- **Engineering is a business lever, not a cost center.** If your engineering org isn't driving outcomes, something is structurally wrong.
- **Speed is the only sustainable competitive advantage.** The team that figures things out fastest is the team that wins.
- **Sincere enthusiasm is contagious.** Culture isn't a handbook — it's what people feel when they show up.

Or, to distill it down: build customer-obsessed teams that own outcomes, not tickets. Hire for curiosity, invest in growth, and create the conditions where people are inspired to do their best work.

## People Who Inspire Me

I draw a lot of inspiration from people who combined deep technical skill with courage — people like **Grace Hopper**, who invented the first compiler and championed the idea that code should read like English. **Margaret Hamilton**, who wrote the Apollo guidance software and coined "software engineering" as a discipline. **Gene Kranz**, the mission control legend who defined what it means to be accountable.

On the philosophical side, I'm a fan of **Slavoj Žižek** — someone who makes philosophy accessible and provocative, and challenges you to question what you take for granted.

And then there are the builders: **Linus Torvalds**, who created Linux and Git — two tools that fundamentally shaped how software is made. **Steve Wozniak**, who built the Apple I and II essentially alone, purely for the joy of it.

## Away From the Keyboard

When I'm not building software or thinking about teams, you'll probably find me in the kitchen chasing a new recipe, skinning uphill or riding a chairlift somewhere in the Sierra, or experiencing live music with friends. I live in San Francisco with my wife and our little one.
